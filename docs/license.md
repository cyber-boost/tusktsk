## Contributor License Agreement (CLA)

### What is the CLA?

The Contributor License Agreement ensures that contributions to TuskLang can be safely used in both open source and commercial contexts.

### CLA Terms

By submitting any contribution (code, documentation, bug reports, etc.), you:

1. **Grant License**: Provide CyberBoost LLC a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free, irrevocable license to use, modify, and distribute your contributions

2. **Represent Authority**: Confirm you have the legal right to grant such license

3. **Warrant Originality**: Ensure your contribution doesn't violate third-party rights

4. **Electronic Acceptance**: Electronic submission constitutes acceptance of these terms

### How to Submit

Contributions are automatically covered by the CLA when you:
- Submit a pull request
- Create an issue
- Contribute to discussions
- Submit documentation

## Profit Sharing Program

### Overview

CyberBoost LLC commits to sharing commercial licensing revenue with active contributors:

- **33%** - Distributed quarterly to Approved Active Contributors
- **33%** - Operational overhead and business expenses
- **33%** - Investments, reserves, and business development
- **1%** - Administrative costs

### Eligibility Criteria

- **Significant Ongoing Contributions**: Regular, meaningful contributions to the project
- **Formal Approval**: Requires approval by CyberBoost LLC
- **Merit-Based Selection**: Quality and impact matter more than quantity
- **Active Status**: Must maintain active contributor status

### Selection Process

1. **Nomination**: Contributors can be nominated by community or self-nominate
2. **Review**: CyberBoost LLC reviews contribution history and impact
3. **Approval**: Formal approval process with clear criteria
4. **Distribution**: Quarterly profit sharing to approved contributors
