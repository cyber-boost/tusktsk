## Incident Classification

### Critical (Immediate Response)
**Definition**: System compromise, data breach, service outage
**Response Time**: < 15 minutes
**Notification**: All primary contacts + executive team

**Examples**:
- Unauthorized system access
- Data exfiltration
- Complete service failure
- Security breach

### High Priority (1 hour)
**Definition**: Performance issues, partial outages, security concerns
**Response Time**: < 1 hour
**Notification**: Security team + relevant leads

**Examples**:
- Performance degradation
- Partial service outage
- Suspicious activity
- Compliance violations

### Medium Priority (4 hours)
**Definition**: Minor issues, maintenance, updates
**Response Time**: < 4 hours
**Notification**: Relevant team leads

**Examples**:
- Minor bugs
- Configuration issues
- Performance optimization
- Routine maintenance

### Low Priority (24 hours)
**Definition**: Documentation, improvements, enhancements
**Response Time**: < 24 hours
**Notification**: Team leads

**Examples**:
- Documentation updates
- Feature requests
- Minor improvements
- Training needs

## Emergency Response Procedures

### Step 1: Incident Detection
1. **Automated Monitoring**: Systems detect anomalies
2. **Manual Reporting**: Team members report issues
3. **External Reports**: Users or partners report problems
4. **Security Alerts**: Security tools trigger alerts

### Step 2: Initial Assessment
1. **Classify Incident**: Determine severity level
2. **Activate Response**: Notify appropriate contacts
3. **Document Details**: Record incident information
4. **Establish Timeline**: Begin incident tracking

### Step 3: Response Execution
1. **Containment**: Isolate affected systems
2. **Investigation**: Determine root cause
3. **Remediation**: Fix the issue
4. **Verification**: Confirm resolution

### Step 4: Communication
1. **Internal Updates**: Keep team informed
2. **External Communication**: Notify stakeholders
3. **Public Relations**: Manage public messaging
4. **Regulatory Reporting**: Comply with requirements

### Step 5: Recovery
1. **System Restoration**: Return to normal operation
2. **Data Recovery**: Restore lost or corrupted data
3. **Service Validation**: Verify all services working
4. **Performance Monitoring**: Track system health

### Step 6: Post-Incident
1. **Documentation**: Complete incident report
2. **Analysis**: Conduct post-mortem review
3. **Lessons Learned**: Identify improvements
4. **Process Updates**: Update procedures

## Escalation Procedures

### Level 1: Team Lead
**Trigger**: Incident detected
**Action**: Initial assessment and response
**Timeframe**: 0-15 minutes

### Level 2: Department Head
**Trigger**: Critical incident or Level 1 escalation
**Action**: Coordinate response and resources
**Timeframe**: 15-60 minutes

### Level 3: Executive Team
**Trigger**: Major incident or Level 2 escalation
**Action**: Strategic decisions and external communication
**Timeframe**: 60+ minutes

### Level 4: Board of Directors
**Trigger**: Catastrophic incident or regulatory requirement
**Action**: Strategic oversight and legal compliance
**Timeframe**: As required
